(f) Executable band and missed adverse selection

The architecture—current-evidence-only, fail closed on structurally absent current shape, one executable distribution, conservative q bounds—is right. The statistical claim of exactness is not. Clopper–Pearson is an exact interval for a binomial sampling model; treating 51 operational ensemble members as 51 independent Bernoulli trials is generally unjustified. Estimate settlement-graded overdispersion and use a beta-binomial bound, or conservatively substitute a statistical effective sample size before CP. Cantelli is valid for true moments but should remain an independent worst-case backstop; plug-in moments from partial or mixed-cycle data first require the variance penalties above. Bare Stats

Also, if serving chooses the tighter of CP and Cantelli bounds, its advertised coverage requires an explicit simultaneous-coverage construction—such as alpha allocation or a union-bound argument. Naïvely taking the tighter bound does not preserve each method’s marginal coverage.
