## PART B — settled-history favorite-capture counterfactual (since 2026-05-29)
- 681 VERIFIED settled families; 45 had an in-band [0.80,0.97] market favorite with books.
- Naive "buy the favorite at latest top-of-book ask" win rate: 31/45 = 68.9%.
- Net P&L per $100 staked: **−21.76** (LOSING).
- INTERPRETATION: buying favorites at the LATEST (often post-collapse, near-1.0) ask is
  −EV — the edge is gone by then. The operator's claim is about MID-WINDOW favorites
  (6h/2h before end) at lower ask, not the terminal book. The probe's "latest book"
  proxy is the conservative (worst) entry; a timed-entry counterfactual is the follow-up.
  Honest disclosure: this part does NOT prove favorite-buying is +EV; it proves the
  current never-enter behavior is not obviously costing money at the terminal book, while
  the mid-window analysis (Part C) is where the missed value concentrates.

## KEY VERDICTS
1. The ranker is ALREADY expected-log-utility (Kelly ΔU), NOT ev_per_dollar — operator
   defect-hypothesis #1 is WRONG at the ranker. BUT with a FLAT A_y baseline and stake ≪
   bankroll, ΔU → EV/B (first-order log expansion), so variance-discrimination is weak:
   it does not preferentially down-rank rings vs favorites the way true Kelly-per-risk would.
2. The favorite is never entered for TWO structural reasons, not a ranking preference:
   (a) lead-day q_lcb is NULL/flat → no bound clears the ask; (b) on settlement day the
   direction law keyed on the STALE lead-time mu* forbids the cheap favorite expression.
3. EV/$ survives ONLY as a sign gate (admission), correctly documented as not-a-ranker.
4. Per-family selection is genuinely TOP-1 (`_select_proof_by_robust_marginal_utility`) —
   confirms operator defect #2 (favorite YES and ring NO of one family cannot both submit).
